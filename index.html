Beginnings

Bergling was a member of the Laidback Luke Forums, where he refined his craft and, at times, demonstrated his distinct deep house style. By 2009 to 2010, Bergling was a prolific producer and was releasing music incredibly quickly. His remixes during this period were "Sound of Now", "Muja", "Ryu" and "Even". Then, in 2010, Bergling released the hit song "Seek Bromance", which reached the top 20 in several countries, including Belgium, France, the Netherlands, United Kingdom, and Sweden.

Levels

In 2011, Bergling released "Levels", which launched him into the mainstream. The song reached the top ten in Austria, Belgium, Bosnia, Croatia, Denmark, Finland, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, the Netherlands, Slovenia and the United Kingdom, whilst topping the charts in Hungary, Norway and Sweden.

Stories

On 2 October 2015 he released his second studio album called "Stories" by PRMD Music and Island Records. It was produced by Avicii along with the collaborations of Salem Al Fakir, Alex Ebert, Carl Falk, Kristoffer Fogelmark, Martin Garrix, Dhani Lennevald, Ash Pournouri, Albin Nedler, and Vincent Pontare on several tracks. It includes 14 singles that are: "Waiting for Love", "Talk to Myself", "Ten More Days", "For a Better Day", "Broken Arrows", "True Believer" "City Lights", "Pure Grinding", "Sunset Jesus", "Can't Catch Me", "Somewhere in Stockholm", "Trouble" and "Gonna Love Ya".

If you want to know more detailed information about his life and musical career go to the Wikipedia entry of Avicii.
